Tuesday 5th September 2023

At approximately 9:30am in the Dorfman Theatre last week, a big and exciting announcement was made by Mr Troen, head of Entrepreneurship at our school. It was potentially our turn to be involved in Young Enterprise.

And so, 15 minutes later a group of 5 was formed. 2 more joined later to become 7. We were told that we had to pitch an idea to the judges on the 18th of September in ‘Dragon’s Den Style.’ The year group was all hyped in excitement, however getting through the first hurdle will not be easy. Indeed, only 3 teams will be given a place. Every break time between then and now did our group meet up. We discussed weird and wacky ideas. Suggestions included automatic recycling bin sorters, phone holders that stuck to your chest, and eye tracking devices that allowed hands-free navigation on your smartphone.

But there’s something that we know for sure. We want to solve a problem. Whether that’s environmental issues, educational issues, cost issues or skill issues, we want to use Young Enterprise as an opportunity to help society in one way or another.

Friday 15th September 2023

After great deals of brainstorming and discussion over the past week, we have come up with an idea - a catalytic converter. How are we going to make it? We’re not particularly sure. However we are keen to show the judges that we have a passion for providing solutions to issues in today’s world, one step at a time. In this case, it’s an air pollution issue.

Earlier at lunch, we made a few last minute edits to the presentation before finally submitting the google form! It’s quite exciting and relieving for sure and all we have to do now, is rehearse our pitch on Monday.

Wednesday 8th November 2023 - Mingyo

As usual, we all met on a Wednesday afternoon to discuss our ideas. We were confident about making a maths game, but the rest of the concept was as blurry as the sky on a cold wet night in Stoke. Ideas flew across the room - I suggested a board game with figurines because I believed that physical parts children can touch can increase attention span and make the game more interesting - stimulating the hand muscles which are linked to brain activities. However, when we looked into the pricings, especially with the ban on Chinese products in mind, the hopes of having physical parts slowly, gradually, faded.

Instead, we decided to focus on a card game with higher quality questions. The ideas were still not unified though with various concepts from a battle-like system like pokemon trading card games to halli-galli style time limiting games or even mystery game where players cooperate to solve a big question. We decided to brainstorm some example questions with the card game as a base and figure out which rule would work best as we make the questions and play-test it.

Saturday 18th November 2023, Evening - Jacob

We stood there at our stall and it seemed like it wouldn’t go very well at all. Our initial tactic to try to get customers was to scream “Very fun game! Guaranteed prizes! Very cool plushies!”

That was until we started approaching people individually asking if they’d be interested. A line we quite often used was “On a scale of one to ten, how good are you at aiming?” which would then lead to encouraging them to take part in the game. The sales tactic that proved most successful however was focusing on the demographic who would be most interested in our stall. We had bubble tea plushies on offer, and therefore approaching people who held bubble teas in their hands or just leaving the bubble tea stand would be the target market. Many did win prizes, but we were making profit. In the SPGS fair, there was a system of tokens where one token was equivalent to one sterling, and we had 100 of these tokens in our cashbox after the first 60 minutes. We had broken even in the first hour.

The fair was a total of 4 hours, and we are fairly confident that it counts up to approximately 400 tokens, which is amazing. This does not even include the 25 Christmas cards that we plan to sell tomorrow at St Paul’s Juniors. “Christmas Cards??” you may ask. Well, two nights ago the fundraising team decided to order 25 Christmas Cards. These christmas cards have digital drawings of the school on them. We also ordered compostable bags around it. We paid 6p each for these, and for that low price it allowed the perceived value of the card to be much more than what it would be without.

Anyways, it’s been an incredibly tiring day for the 6 members of the team who were there for the full 4 hours or for half of it.

Wednesday 13th December 2023 - Aneesh, R&D Team Member

At last, what I would call an exhausting yet enjoyably stimulating hour is now over. Half of our questions have been created, ready for a thorough check and, hopefully, an approval! Of course, I am fully aware that the questions we have created will not be the finalised versions that will bring simultaneous entertainment and educational growth to our customers once our product is released, but I feel a wave of pride and accomplishment, having achieved this significant milestone. A lot of careful thought, design, and planning have gone into the 25 questions we just finished creating, as well as coming up with creative and interesting pop-culture references, which will hopefully address our target market appropriately.

While creating these questions, we combined simple mathematical logic with entertaining references and jokes, in order to achieve the enhancement of the problems - who wants cards with regular exam questions on them? For example, we made a Christmas-themed question, in which Santa was running late, and had to divide his remaining gifts between a number of children, as well as a harder question in which there is a virus that replicates itself once every few seconds, forming an introductory link to exponents.

In terms of the process of creating the questions itself, one of the key techniques used was reverse-engineering; we started off with the final answer, and worked our way backwards to determine a set of possible questions that could lead to said answer. Furthermore, it is important to note that this process not only enabled us to progress as a business, but also engaged our brains, forcing us to move out of our comfort zone and be creative, as well as analytical, since we selected the best questions that seemed to have the highest practicality and the least likelihood to cause confusion or other unforeseen problems. Overall, the experience was highly delightful, and we hope that we will successfully be able to share our passion for maths and enable other children to rapidly progress through our questions.
